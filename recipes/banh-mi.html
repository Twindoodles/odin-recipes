How to make a banh mi

A remnant of French colonialism, banh mi is loved globally and locally. Now you can make one yourself if you ever find yourself craving one.

Recipe is pulled from Hungry Huy

Ingredients

  • 4 to 6 Vietnamese baguettes
  • 1 to 1.5 lb homemade pork belly or
  • 1 to 1.5 lb grilled pork
  • 2-3 crunchy cucumbers
  • Mayonnaise
  • Vietnamese pate
  • Homemade Vietnamese pickles / Đồ chua – as much as you'd like
  • 8 to 12 sprigs cilantro
  • Maggi or soy sauce
  • 1 to 2 medium jalapeños thinly sliced
  • 1 medium yellow onion thinly sliced, optional
  • freshly ground black pepper

Steps

  1. Toast baguettes to warm, but not so crispy that they fall apart when you split them to add fillings.
  2. Wash all the veggies and slice to prep. Slicing with a mandoline will keep everything uniform.
  3. Cut baguettes in half but leave the back part uncut so the loaf stays intact
  4. Add mayo and/or pate to the bottom, then layer the meat and veggies to your liking
  5. Add a few shakes of Maggi or soy sauce, and a few grinds of pepper to taste
